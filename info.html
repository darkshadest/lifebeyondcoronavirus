What is coronavirus?

Coronavirus is a contagious virus that has become a pandemic during the year 2020.

Most cases are very mild, but more serious cases can lead to severe lung damage. This disease is thought to spread mostly through close contact, hand to hand, and hand to object to hand. Examples of these types of contact are:

  • Close contact- infected person coughs, sneezes, and a person up to around 6ft away breathes in microscopic droplets
  • Hand to hand- handshakes, fist bumps, etc.
  • Hand to Object to Hand- person touches infected surface, then touches a part of the body with the same hand

Scientists are researching how long stays on surfaces, and other ways of spread. A study from China shows that Coronavirus has 1.4% mortality rate, but the actual rate may be lower, and closer to that of the flu because there are likely many cases we don't know about(because they could be mild cases, or show no symptoms). The flu only has a 0.1% mortality rate. Older people or people with ailments are more at risk for severe cases, due to their lungs being weak, while children are safer. Right now, there is a shortage of test kits, and when there are more, we will have a better idea of how widespread this virus is. With more test kits, we will be able to screen for active cases and evidence of past infection. Recently, there have been a small number of cases from community spread- meaning it is of unknown origin

SYMPTOMS

Usual Symptoms

  • Cough
  • Shortness of breath or difficulty breathing
  • Fever
  • Chills
  • Muscle pain
  • Sore throat
  • New loss of taste or smell

Severe Symptoms

  • Trouble breathing
  • Persistent pain or pressure in the chest
  • New confusion
  • Inability to wake or stay awake
  • Bluish lips or face
